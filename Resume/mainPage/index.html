The First Flight

On December 17, 1903, Wilbur and Orville Wright succeeded in flying the first free, controlled flight of a power-driven, heavier-than-air plane. Wilbur flew their plane for 59 seconds, over a distance of 852 feet, an extraordinary achievement.

The Contribution of War to Flight

Airplanes were an integral and versatile instrument in warfare during World War II. The Allied and Axis Power militaries used planes as bombers, fighters, reconnaissance planes, and transport planes, designing multiple models for each of these purposes.

Early Commercial Flight

As technology improved, aircraft evolved into sleek, high-performance modern airliners. A solid infrastructure took shape under government guidance through the Post Office and the Commerce Department, and regulatory reforms reshaped the industry.











Modern Flight

Modern flight has transformed the world we live in, it has shrunk the time it takes to travel, bringing us closer together and allowing communication and collaboration like we have never been able to before.
